## Context Setting
Establish what was already done before this A/B test. What are some questions you can ask to get more information on what has already been measured, analyzed, or attempted? List them below...

#The goal here is to understand prior work, assumptions, and the foundation of this analysis.

1. What business problem or decision is driving this analysis request?
2. What was the primary objective of the A/B test?
3. What hypothesis was defined at the start of the experiment?
4. Has similar analysis been conducted previously? If so, what were the key findings or outcomes?
5. What metrics or KPIs have already been measured or evaluated?
6. What does an “ideal outcome” look like from a business or product perspective?
7. What time frame does this dataset cover?
8. How was this data collected?
9. Are there any known data quality issues or limitations that could impact interpretation?
10. What tools, methods, or frameworks were previously used in the analysis?
11. What assumptions were made going into the experiment?
12. One finding suggests that lower word count increased viewing time, does this align with observed user behavior outside of the data?


## Goal Alignment 
Align with stakeholders on what success means before interpreting any numbers.

#The goal here is to ensure that success criteria and stakeholder expectations are clearly defined.

1. Which KPIs are most critical for evaluating success?
2. Which KPIs are you directly responsible for?
3. How is success currently defined for this project?
4. What does exceeding expectations look like in measurable terms?
5. Are there specific targets we should aim to meet or surpass?
6. How do you intend to use the results of this analysis moving forward?
7. What decisions, strategies, or actions should this analysis inform?
8. Who is the primary audience for the final deliverable? 
9. What level of detail is expected in the output?
10. How will we evaluate whether this analysis delivered meaningful impact?

## Define Over-performance 
Consider what it would mean if the treatment dramatically exceeded expectations.

#The goal here is to help clarify how to interpret outcomes that exceed expectations.

1. What would over-performance look like for this experiment?
2. If results significantly exceed expectations, how should that influence current strategy or next steps?
3. If we focus on improving this one KPI, could we be negatively impacting something else?
4. How do we know these results are real and not just temporary or random?


## Data Sourcing
Reflect on what other data sources you can use to answer your stakeholders exploratory goals.

#The goal here is to identify opportunities to strengthen the analysis through additional context

1. Are there additional datasets that could provide more context?
2. Can we combine this with other data for more insight?
3. Are there qualitative insights that can support or challenge the findings?
4. Are there industry standards we can compare this to?



#By being clear on the context, goals, and data from the beginning, we keep the analysis focused and make sure it actually supports real business decisions. This helps us move forward with clarity and deliver insights that are both accurate and useful.
